# Rubric: Brazil Public Money Trail

Score submissions on whether they help a citizen inspect the path from policy activity to public money without overclaiming.

## Reward

- Careful evidence chains across official sources.
- Correct legislative timeline and public spending summaries.
- Meaningful joins across theme, agency, actor, program, time window, and documented identifiers.
- Explicit separation between verified facts, direct documented links, hypotheses, weak signals, and unsupported speculation.
- Transparent uncertainty and data-coverage limitations.
- Civic usefulness for journalists, auditors, researchers, or citizens.
- Visual clarity: timelines, graph placeholders, map, rankings, claims/evidence tables.
- Reproducible scripts and source manifests.

## Penalize

- Implies corruption or illegal conduct without direct authoritative evidence.
- Treats same-theme proximity as proof of influence.
- Confuses correlation with causation.
- Fails to cite official records.
- Hides mock data or missing API tokens.
- Hallucinates politicians, agencies, suppliers, spending records, or audit findings.
